# Privacy Policy Draft

This draft is a working document for Count On Us.

It is not legal advice and should be reviewed by qualified counsel before publication or submission.

## 1. Introduction

Count On Us ("we", "us", "our") provides a Shopify app that helps merchants track production costs, estimate donation pools, manage cause allocations, record disbursements, publish donation receipts, and generate related reporting.

This Privacy Policy explains what information we process, how we use it, when we share it, how long we retain it, and the choices available to merchants and data subjects.

## 2. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to information processed through the Count On Us app, our support channels, and any related public pages we provide, such as support, privacy, receipt, or data-processing request pages.

## 3. Roles

For merchant and customer data processed through the app:

- the merchant generally acts as the data controller
- Count On Us generally acts as a data processor or service provider on the merchant's behalf

For data we process to operate our own business, such as billing, security, support, and compliance records, we may act as a controller.

## 4. Information We Process

Depending on how the app is used, we may process the following categories of information:

### Merchant and shop information

- shop name
- shop domain and Shopify identifiers
- staff/account information made available through Shopify
- settings, configuration, and preferences
- support and administrative contacts

### Product, catalog, and configuration information

- products, variants, templates, materials, equipment, and cost configuration records
- cause assignments and related donation configuration
- provider-connection configuration and mapping metadata

### Order and reporting information

- Shopify order identifiers and related order metadata needed for donation tracking
- order snapshots and reporting records
- disbursement records, cause allocation records, and tax-estimation records
- uploaded receipt files and receipt metadata

### Customer-related information

Where necessary to provide app functionality, we may process limited customer-related information associated with orders, receipts, donation emails, public transparency surfaces, or compliance handling.

We aim to minimize the customer information processed and only use what is needed to deliver the app's features.

### Support, security, and operations information

- support requests and correspondence
- log data and operational diagnostics
- job execution metadata
- audit and security event records

## 5. How We Use Information

We use information to:

- provide the app's core donation-management and reporting features
- calculate and store estimates, snapshots, reporting records, and disbursement data
- display donation and transparency information in merchant-configured storefront experiences
- send post-purchase donation summary emails when enabled by the merchant
- publish merchant-approved receipt or transparency information where configured
- troubleshoot, secure, maintain, and improve the app
- respond to legal, privacy, and compliance requests

## 6. Legal Bases

When we act as controller, we rely on legal bases such as:

- performance of a contract
- legitimate interests in operating, securing, and improving the service
- compliance with legal obligations
- consent, where required

When we act as processor on behalf of merchants, the merchant is generally responsible for determining the lawful basis for processing personal data through the app.

## 7. Sharing and Disclosure

We may share information with service providers and subprocessors that help us operate the app, such as hosting, storage, email delivery, monitoring, and support providers.

We may also disclose information:

- when required by law
- to protect the security or integrity of the app
- in connection with corporate transactions, subject to applicable safeguards

We do not sell merchant or customer personal information.

## 8. Public and Merchant-Configured Content

Some app features allow merchants to make certain information publicly available, such as:

- donation receipts
- public transparency summaries
- cause information

These public features are controlled by merchant configuration. Merchants are responsible for deciding what content they choose to publish through the app.

## 9. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as needed to provide the app, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, and maintain appropriate business and security records.

Where the app or our published documentation states a more specific retention/deletion timeline, such as uninstall-related deletion windows, those operational timelines should be read together with this policy.

## 10. Uninstall and Deletion

When a merchant uninstalls the app, we follow our documented uninstall and deletion workflow, which may include:

- immediate or near-immediate cleanup of Shopify-hosted app resources
- scheduled deletion of app database records
- scheduled deletion of stored receipt files or similar hosted assets
- limited retention of records required for security, legal, or compliance purposes

The exact operational timing should remain consistent with the app's actual implementation and merchant-facing documentation.

## 11. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect information against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## 12. International Transfers

If information is transferred across borders, we will use appropriate safeguards where required by applicable law.

## 13. Merchant and Data Subject Requests

Depending on applicable law and our role in the processing, merchants and data subjects may have rights relating to access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or objection.

Requests may be submitted through our support or privacy/compliance contact path. Where we act as processor for a merchant, we may direct the requester to the relevant merchant when appropriate.

## 14. Data Processing Addendum Requests

Merchants that require a Data Processing Addendum may request one using our published DPA request path.

## 15. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the effective date and revise the published text accordingly.

## 16. Contact

Privacy and data-protection questions should be directed to the privacy/compliance contact published on our support or privacy pages.
