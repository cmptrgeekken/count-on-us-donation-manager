# Data Processing Addendum Draft

This draft is a working document for Count On Us.

It is not legal advice and should be reviewed by qualified counsel before use.

## Data Processing Addendum

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the agreement between:

**Controller:**  
[Merchant legal entity]

and

**Processor:**  
[Your legal entity operating Count On Us]

## 1. Purpose

This DPA governs the processing of personal data by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the Count On Us Shopify app.

## 2. Subject Matter and Duration

Processor will process personal data for the duration of Controller's use of Count On Us and any documented retention/deletion period that applies after termination, uninstall, or service end.

## 3. Nature and Purpose of Processing

Processor provides app functionality related to:

- production-cost tracking
- donation allocation and cause configuration
- order snapshotting and reporting
- disbursement logging and receipt handling
- public transparency and receipt publication features where configured
- post-purchase donation summary emails where enabled
- support, security, and operational maintenance

## 4. Categories of Data Subjects

Depending on the merchant's use of the app, data subjects may include:

- merchant staff/users
- store customers
- cause or organization contacts, if provided by the merchant
- support contacts

## 5. Types of Personal Data

Depending on the merchant's use of the app, personal data may include:

- shop and account identifiers
- contact information
- order-related identifiers and limited customer/order metadata
- uploaded receipt files and related metadata
- support communications
- operational logs and diagnostics

## 6. Controller Obligations

Controller is responsible for:

- determining the lawful basis for processing
- providing any required notices to data subjects
- ensuring that instructions to Processor are lawful
- using the app in compliance with applicable privacy laws

## 7. Processor Obligations

Processor shall:

- process personal data only on documented instructions from Controller, except where otherwise required by law
- ensure that persons authorized to process personal data are subject to confidentiality obligations
- implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- assist Controller, where applicable, with data subject requests
- assist Controller, where applicable, with security, breach, and compliance obligations
- delete or return personal data at the end of services, subject to applicable legal retention requirements
- make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA

## 8. Subprocessors

Controller authorizes Processor to use subprocessors for hosting, storage, email delivery, monitoring, and related app operations, provided Processor remains responsible for their compliance as required by law.

Processor should maintain and provide subprocessor information through its privacy/compliance documentation or request process.

## 9. International Transfers

If Processor transfers personal data across borders, Processor will use an appropriate transfer mechanism where required by applicable law.

## 10. Security

Processor will maintain reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards appropriate to the risks presented by the processing.

## 11. Data Subject Requests and Assistance

Taking into account the nature of the processing, Processor will provide reasonable assistance to Controller in responding to data subject requests and privacy/compliance obligations where required by law.

## 12. Deletion and Return

Upon termination, uninstall, or end of services, Processor will follow its documented deletion/retention workflow, including deletion or scheduled deletion of app data and stored receipt files, except where retention is required by law or necessary for legitimate security/compliance purposes.

## 13. Audit and Information Rights

Processor will provide Controller with information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, subject to appropriate confidentiality, security, and scope limitations.

## 14. Order of Precedence

If there is a conflict between this DPA and another agreement governing the processing of personal data, this DPA controls to the extent of that conflict.
